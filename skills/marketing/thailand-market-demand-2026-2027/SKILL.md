# Thailand Market Demand & Business Outlook (2026-2027)

## Overview
This skill contains structured macroeconomic data, rising/falling market trends, and framework-level guidelines to analyze consumer demand in Thailand for the years 2026 and 2027. It is compiled from primary research reports of leading Thai research centers (Kasikorn Research and SCB EIC) to provide quantitative and qualitative bases for business decision-making.

## Consumer Behavior & Buying Constraints
1. **Severely Weakened Purchasing Power**:
   - High cost of living driven by energy prices.
   - Household debt remains high at **5x of monthly income** (avg. monthly income ~28,151 THB).
   - Banks are heavily restricting credit lines (especially for retail automotive and mortgage loans).
2. **Shift to "Rental/Subscription" Models**:
   - In properties and automobiles, consumers with low liquidity choose renting/leasing instead of committing to long-term ownership.
3. **Preference for Green/Clean Tech**:
   - Rapidly growing interest in EVs and energy-saving appliances (solar panels/clean energy packages) due to soaring electricity and fuel costs.

## Sector-Specific Analysis

### 1. Automotive & Mobility
* **Overall Market**: Expected to drop slightly to **620,000 units** (-0.2% YoY).
* **Winners (BEV & PHEV)**:
  - Forecasted to expand **+28% YoY** to **181,000 units** (29% market share).
  - Chinese car brands dominate BEV/PHEV sales, driving Chinese dealership networks to grow **+10% YoY** (reaching ~730 dealers).
* **Losers (ICE/HEV & Pickups)**:
  - Commercial vehicles (pickups) are down **-5.0% YoY** due to credit restrictions.
  - Japanese/Western dealerships decline by **-4.0% YoY** (to ~1,520 dealers). Low-market-share brands (1%-4% share) risk a **10.8% YoY** closure rate.

### 2. Real Estate & Housing
* **Overall Market**: Unit transfers expected to shrink **-5.1% YoY** to 300,000 units (4th consecutive year of decline).
* **Key Dynamics**:
  - **Second-hand homes** perform better (-4.7% YoY) and represent **64.6%** of total transfers.
  - **Foreign Condo Purchases**: Growing slowly at **+1.8% YoY** to 15,200 units (representing 5% of total market).
  - Developers rely heavily on price wars and extended promotions to clear inventory.
  - Anticipation of government LTV relaxations and transfer fee waivers for homes under 7M THB to support the H2 market.

### 3. Retail, Food & Beverage
* **Food & Beverage Retail**: Expected to grow by **2.5% YoY** but overall profitability declines due to input raw material cost hikes.
* **Exports**: F&B exports slow down to **+2.0% YoY**. Food processor companies face strict competition from cheap Chinese imports (especially in Lecithin and MSG products, which dropped -9.4% and -6.2% respectively).

### 4. Tech & Electronics (AI Boom)
* **AI & Silicon Trade**: Strong export driver. Chip exports forecasted to grow **+22% YoY** in 2026.
* **Electrical Converters**: Exports to the US expected to grow **+18.9% YoY** as the US diversifies supply lines.
* **Solar Module Exports**: Hard hit by global tax adjustments and China's tax waiver cancellations on solar components; Thai exports expected to shrink **-59% to -68% YoY**.

## Common Pitfalls when Analyzing Thai Demand
1. **Overestimating Retail Recovery**: Analysts often expect a fast bounce-back after government stimulus. However, structural debt (5x of income) acts as a persistent cap.
2. **Treating Tourism as a Unified Sector**: High-end tourism (hotels, global events in Q4) remains resilient, while middle-to-low-tier tourism suffers from flight reductions and geopolitical constraints.
3. **Assuming ICE-to-EV Transition is Friendly for All**: The transition is displacing Japanese/Western dealer networks, leading to systemic retail automotive layoffs.

## Verification Checklist for Demand Analysis
- [ ] Has household debt ratio (5x income limit) been factored into target segment's purchasing power?
- [ ] Is the business model heavily dependent on bank financing? If so, have tight credit guards (-5% commercial vehicle, -5.1% property transfers) been adjusted?
- [ ] Does the product compete with cheap Chinese imports? (Check tariff structures and alternative market options).
- [ ] For export products, has the impact of geopolitical conflicts (Red Sea / Hormuz Strait routes raising freight costs by 2-3x) been calculated?
